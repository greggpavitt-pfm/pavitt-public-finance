IPSAS Adoption Checklist for Ministries of Finance

A practical 12-point guide to the steps a ministry must complete before going live with accrual IPSAS, with notes on common pitfalls.

Pavitt Public Finance · pfmexpert.net · April 2026

Pre-transition (Months -12 to -6)

  1. Lock the data model

    Map your existing chart of accounts to IPSAS line items before any system change. Mid-build remapping forces rework across IFMIS configuration, reports, and training material.

  2. Inventory the standards in scope

    First-time adoption uses IPSAS 33 plus the standards effective in your transition year. Watch the IPSAS 46/47/48 cluster for periods on or after 1 January 2026.

  3. Identify the IPSAS 33 exemptions you will use — and the deadlines for closing each one

    IPSAS 33 grants temporary relief from full retrospective application. Each exemption has a maximum window. Document the closing date for every exemption claimed.

  4. Capture the opening balance

    Decide the measurement basis per asset class: deemed cost, fair value, or historical cost. Document the rationale per class. Audit will ask.

Transition build (Months -6 to 0)

  1. Decide your transition disclosures

    IPSAS 33 requires a reconciliation from the previous basis to IPSAS for the opening statement of financial position. Plan the format and the level of disaggregation.

  2. Map your IFMIS to the new line items

    Including budget line mapping for IPSAS 24 budget-comparison disclosures. Test the mapping with a real prior-period dataset, not synthetic data.

  3. Train preparers and reviewers

    Minimum 40 hours per finance officer. Do not rely on three-day workshops alone. Self-paced modules with assessments give a stronger audit trail of competency.

  4. Document accounting policies

    A short-form policy manual approved by the CFO. One page per topic is enough. Auditors prefer concise, decision-focused policies over long copy-paste from the standards.

Cut-over (Month 0)

  1. Walk through one full reporting cycle in parallel

    Old basis and IPSAS side-by-side for at least one quarter. Differences must be explainable line-by-line.

  2. Get audit pre-engagement

    Auditor sign-off on opening balances before going live. Do not wait until the year-end audit to surface measurement disagreements.

  3. Set the cut-over date — and the date by which all amendments must be closed

    Open issues left unresolved at cut-over almost always require restatement in the first IPSAS-basis statements.

Post-implementation (Months +6 to +12)

  1. Plan the post-implementation review

    Month 6 and month 12. Track: opening balance adjustments, IFMIS mapping issues, training gaps, audit findings, and disclosure clarifications. Feed lessons into your IPSAS policy manual.
